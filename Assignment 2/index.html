HAPPINESS LEVEL AROUND THE WORLD
To measure the well-being of population around the world (2015-2021)

Happiness Level of the population are important in any countries in the world as they can be a very good parameter to estimate the well-being of the population in a country. The Happiness Score used in this visualisation is estimated by using several factors that represent the well-being of the population in a country as estimators. The estimators are as follows: GDP per capita, Healthy Life Expectancy, Social support, Freedom to make life choices, Generosity and Corruption Perception.

INTERACTIVE WORLD MAP TO VISUALISE THE DATA

From the world map showing the Happiness Level over the world, it can be seen that countries from Central Africa and Asia had the lowest overall happiness level, with a darker colour scheme around the regions, and it continued to decrease as year increases. On the other hand, countries on the Northern Europe, North America and Australia had been showing high happiness level throughout the years, with a brighter colour scheme around the regions.

COMPARISON BETWEEN COUNTRIES

With that, the best countries with the highest happiness scores are shown in the bar chart on the below. Countries with higher rankings in happiness score are more possible to be advanced countries, hence the population will have better well-being. This can be observed on the scatterplot on the side, which the country with the highest happiness level is always on the far right, with high GDP per capita and high life expectancy. Besides that, countries having high happiness scores such as Switzerland, Norway and Finland are located close to each other on the world map. From that, it is possible to speculate that geographical location might also have an effect on happiness level.

COMPARISON BETWEEN DIFFERENT YEARS

Year 2019 and Year 2021 are chosen to compare the difference between the factors that are used to determine the well-being of the population. From the chart below, it is possible to observe that the rising of happiness level is caused by the increment of GDP per capita and the Freedom to make life choices of the population. However, there is a vast drop in the Healthy life expectancy, which can be caused by the pandemic that were still on-going during the year.
